Welcome to Algorithms Rocks

Many of those in computer science and mathematics unintentionally (or intenntionaly) make the tools of their trade inaccessable to others. Through simplifying the language, providing introductary example algorithms it is possible to introduce those with minimal understanding to algorithms. By providing an introduction to algorithms in this method you can attract a younger generation into computer science, as well as not exclude those who have not been coding their whole lives.

With this in mind, I am creating Algorithms Rock a website dedicated to simplifying and making algorithms accessable to everyone. Feel free to add your own simple algorithms, explanation, and examples. I intend to eventually write a book for a basic introduction to algorithms titled: Everyday Algorithms. Currently, I have only a few basic algorithms, however over the next couple years I hope to build it out.

If someone wishes to contribute to Algorithms Rock, please feel free to checkout the repo, add your page, and merge. Or if you are Github adverse, feel free to email me at austin at _ agw.io (replace at with @ and remove _).
